What is Yajna?

Yajna is chanting of specific Vedic Mantras sound) by trained and dedicated Vedic pandits. The pandits may make offerings of flowers, ghee, camphor and other things, depending on the type of yajna.

Are there any other names for Yajnas?

Yajna are also called yagya. Yagya is a north Indian term while Yajna is a south Indian word.

How is a Havan performed?

A JAP is performed by the pandits for 125,000 times . This JAP consist of the chanting of mantras on the count of 108 beads of a necklace. This JAP is followed by a HAVAN which is performed for 10% as a JAP , Havan is followed by a Tarpan being performed for 1250 times which is the dashans of Havan. Tarpan is the process of offering JAL to the planets. Then Margin is performed for 125 times which is the dashans of Tarpan. Margin is the processing which the effect of the mantras are entered into the body and life of the Yajna (clients).
